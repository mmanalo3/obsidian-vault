# Types of Notes
## Fleeting Notes
- appear out of no where
- goal is not to organize but to capture
- use inbox
- no structure, formatting, clarity
- for review later on to refine what makes sense
- raw draft of thinking
- quick and temporary ideas

## Literature Notes
- while consuming content
- write in your own words not to copy but translate what stood out
- shows where knowledge came from
- save what truly makes sense
- from external sources

## Permanent Notes
- ideas are taken from fleeting and literature
- should contain only one idea
- should be fully understood even after long time
- consolidated knowledge

# What is truly worth keeping?
- information is not knowledge
- consuming is not the same as learning
- does this really add something to what i already know?
- does this idea connect to one of my goals? or to something i am studying right now?
- if no, not worth keeping
- capture not accumulate, be selective, store less

## Your filters:
- **Keep what makes you think** if an idea triggered reflection, changed your opinion, or connected with another note -- it's worth keeping
- **Keep what you intend to use** if it's something you want to apply to a project, video, class, or piece of writing -- keep it
- **Keep what repeats itself** if an idea shows up in different contexts, it's probably important. That usually means its a core concept connecting multiple areas of knowledge

# A good connection
- Complementary
- Practical example
- Counterpoint
- Thematic

# Transformation
- Consume - input, ideas come in
	- Books, videos, podcasts, observations, experiences
- Reflect & write - processing stage, connecting thoughts
- Produce - output, create something new
	- Text, content, projects, products
